\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}

The evaluation of large language models (LLMs) on structured reasoning tasks presents significant methodological challenges. Traditional metrics such as exact-match accuracy or execution accuracy provide aggregate measures but fail to reveal the underlying failure mechanisms---or more precisely, the protocol-defined failure patterns---that characterize model behavior.

We propose the Checklist-Protocol-Fit-Condition (CPFC) protocol as a principled framework for determining when ordinal verification-entropy analysis is methodologically justified. Our approach provides an ordinal verification-entropy index, not an absolute entropy measure, that captures the distribution of checkpoint-level outcomes.

Importantly, we do not claim that our method reveals the true mechanism of model failure. Rather, we characterize protocol-defined failure patterns that are observable through checkpoint-frequency analysis. The framework is a within-protocol diagnostic, not a universal evaluation instrument.
